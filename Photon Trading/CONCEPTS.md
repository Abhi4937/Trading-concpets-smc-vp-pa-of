## 0. The Big Picture

Photon Trading is a **smart-money / supply-and-demand day-trading school** for **forex majors (mainly EUR/USD)**, built as a **mechanical, rules-based framework**. The single most-repeated rule:

> **Trade FROM strong structure, TARGET weak structure, in line with order flow.**

Their thesis for success has three legs (all required): **Method (a proven-profitable strategy) + Money Management + Mindset.** Early on, weight the strategy; once you're scaling funded capital, ~80–90% of the difference is psychology.

Why price moves (their order-flow basis): not "more buyers than sellers" but **order-flow imbalance** — aggressive market orders consume all passive limit liquidity at a level, forcing price to jump to find more. Institutions can't hide these → they leave **impulsive-candle footprints** = supply/demand zones. The 4-step cycle they trade: **Range (accumulation) → Initiation (impulse) → Mitigation (pullback to zone) → Continuation.**

Style: **~10 trades/month** (not high-frequency), ~70–80% mechanical / 20–30% discretion (the "sweet spot" that beats an algo), London session for day trading.

## 1. MARKET STRUCTURE

- **Bullish** = higher highs + higher lows; **bearish** = lower highs + lower lows. Always draw structure **from the wicks**.
- **Break of Structure (BOS)** = price takes out the prior swing point. **After every BOS, expect a pullback** on that timeframe.
- **Swing high** = the high that *caused* the break of a low; **swing low** = the low that *caused* the break of a high (only confirmable after the next BOS).
- **Strong vs weak (the targeting engine):** "A low's job is to make a high; a high's job is to make a low." A **strong low** broke a high (did its job → will be defended); a **weak high** failed to take the low (→ will be run). **Trade from strong → target weak.**
- **Internal vs swing structure:** internal = the minor highs/lows *inside* the current swing range.

### The 3-tier model (their "Elite" refinement)
1. **Fractal structure** (smallest) → its change = **CHoCH (change of character)**.
2. **Internal structure** → its break = **iBOS** (internal BOS).
3. **Swing structure** (largest, "King").

Logic: a **CHoCH** only signals the start/end of an *internal* pullback (early warning, many false signals); an **iBOS** signals the start/end of a *swing* pullback (real confirmation). Internal structure shifts to *facilitate* the swing pullback — once it drags price to premium/discount and mitigates the HTF zone, its objective is done and it realigns with swing.

### Confirmation rules
- **Conservative (candle close beyond level)** → used for **swing BOS** / trend change.
- **Aggressive (wick through level)** → used for **CHoCH / internal** signals.
- A **wick through a high that fails to close = a liquidity grab, not a BOS.**
- **Trend change** needs the *swing* high/low taken; some wait for a **double BOS** (both a new LL and a new LH) to filter liquidity grabs (he uses double-BOS confirmation for counter-trend trades).

## 2. MULTI-TIMEFRAME ("the secret sauce")

- **"A run on a higher timeframe is a trend on a lower timeframe."** Each HTF impulse leg = a full LTF trend.
- LTFs **switch direction to facilitate** the HTF pullback (4H due to pull back → 15M turns bearish to create it).
- **A CHoCH on one timeframe ≈ a BOS/trend-change on the next timeframe down.**
- **Deeper entry timeframe = less confirmation, more false signals.** Higher = fewer entries, more reliable.

### His timeframe stack & process
- **Weekly/Daily** = longer-term trend context (daily tells you when the 4H is likely to turn).
- **4H = main higher timeframe / the narrative** — "Am I trading the 4H continuation or the 4H pullback?"
- **M15 = the workhorse** — immediate intraday bias *and* where the tradeable zones (POIs) live. **~90% of intraday decisions = follow M15 internal structure.**
- **M1/M5 = execution only** — drop to it at the last second to time entry.
- Always **top-down**, read price "like a book from the start"; lock a fixed timeframe set so you don't timeframe-hop to fit a bias. If misaligned/choppy → **stand aside.**

## 3. PREMIUM / DISCOUNT

- Tool: a **Fibonacci showing only 0 / 0.5 / 1**, drawn swing low → swing high. **0.5 = equilibrium (EQ) = fair value.**
- **Discount** = bottom 50% (buy zone); **Premium** = top 50% (sell zone). Applied fractally on every timeframe.
- Higher-probability longs come from **demand in discount**; shorts from **supply in premium**. Also used to manage counter-trend trades (bank profit aggressively as price reaches EQ/discount, then flip to the pro-trend side).

## 4. SUPPLY & DEMAND

### The 3 zone types (drawing)
1. **Range-created zone** — consolidation then impulsive break; draw top-to-bottom of the range.
2. **Pivot zone** — 1–2 candle pivot:
   - **Demand ("sell-to-buy")** = a candle whose next thrust candle **closes above its high**.
   - **Supply ("buy-to-sell")** = a candle whose next thrust candle **closes below its low**.
   - He's not strict on base-candle color — just wants the thrust to **close beyond** the prior candle.
3. **Fractal zones** (LTF zones showing as wicks/inside bars) — he recommends **ignoring these for now**; use same-timeframe pivot/range zones (most orders).
- **Fractal nature:** a range zone on one timeframe = a pivot zone on the next timeframe up.
- **Refinement trade-off:** range → pivot → wick = smaller stop / higher R:R but lower fill chance. **Default to the single-candle pivot** (best balance). Stay consistent.

### The 8 criteria for a high-probability institutional zone
1. **Did it cause a Break of Structure?** (the strongest filter; **swing > internal > fractal**).
2. **Is it a flip zone** (visible reaction → failed reaction)?
3. **Did it sweep/grab liquidity** on creation? (institutional footprint)
4. **Is there inducement** (liquidity in front of the zone to fill against)? **No inducement / straight push = trap → fails.**
5. **Stacked with a HTF zone** (more orders).
6. **HTF alignment** (more aligned timeframes = higher probability).
7. **Well priced** (demand in discount, supply in premium).
8. **Unmitigated / fresh** (untouched = strongest reaction). **Prioritize the unmitigated extreme of a leg** — "you're only wrong once."

### Flip zones
A valid zone that broke structure and, on return, shows a **reaction → fails to continue → opposite side overpowers** (the **Failed Reaction / FR**, confirmed by a candle close). Draw from top-to-bottom of the reaction. **Flip-vs-extreme rule:** if the flip swept liquidity → take the flip; if not → take the extreme zone (unswept flips are often traps).

## 5. TRADE SETUPS (entry / stop / target)

### The repeatable workflow (blank chart → trade)
1. Top TF → mark swing high/low (current range).
2. Decide 4H **continuation or pullback**.
3. Mark **premium/discount** (0/0.5/1 fib).
4. Find **high-probability POIs** (8 criteria) — demand in discount / supply in premium.
5. Drop to **M15** for bias + executable zones; set **price alerts** on POIs.
6. On a tap, drop to **M1/M5**, wait for the order-flow shift to time entry.

### Bread-and-butter pro-trend long
- 4H/daily bullish + pulled back into **discount** + tapped a **fresh demand zone** that caused a BOS + swept liquidity / has inducement.
- Wait for **M15 to flip bullish** (CHoCH → iBOS / double BOS), then a **M15 pullback into discount**.
- **Entry** (3 generic methods): (a) limit order on the zone; (b) reversal candle at the zone (best with a liquidation/sweep); (c) LTF break of structure for confirmation + better R:R. (The exact M1 model is held for paid members.)
- **Stop:** below the zone / strong low. **Minimum 2-pip stop** on EUR/USD (never below ~1.5).
- **Target:** at minimum the **weak high** (structural liquidity), then keep following internal ranges.

### Counter-trend pullback (his 25R EUR/USD example)
Weekly demand → daily demand → 4H demand extreme (broke structure, swept liquidity) → 4H CHoCH → M15 double-BOS → equal lows (inducement) → discount. Entry ~7am Frankfurt on M1, 2-pip stop. **Bank most volume at the nearest weak M15 structure (~10R+)**, exit remainder near London close — don't swing counter-trend for the fences.

### Management philosophy
Default to **fixed-R** (e.g., always 3R or ~5R, set-and-forget, full size, no partials) to minimize decision fatigue. **Hands-off:** mechanical break-even trigger only; don't exit on how it "looks." Hold for big R only when you've caught a strong low/high with the swing-pullback objective complete and full HTF alignment.

### The 5 common S&D mistakes (their do-not list)
1. Trading from **weak structure** (zoom out; strong → weak).
2. Trading **against the objective** (buying a strong internal low after the swing pullback already mitigated premium supply).
3. **Wrong expectations** — old zones are only *reaction points*, not new trends, once order flow flips.
4. Trading zones with **no inducement** (straight-push legs → fail).
5. **FOMO-ing continuations right after a BOS** (expect the pullback first).

## 6. LIQUIDITY / SMART-MONEY CONCEPTS

- **Liquidity grabs/sweeps** = wick beyond a high/low with no close (institutions sweeping stops to fill against opposing liquidity). A zone that swept on formation = institutional footprint.
- **Inducement** = liquidity deliberately built in front of a zone (equal highs/lows, double tops, trendlines, session/PDH-PDL levels). Require liquidity to the left (in the leg) OR right (in the pullback); none → the zone usually gets swept/fails.
- **High vs low resistance liquidity:** uncleared corrective lows = low resistance → shallow pullback, short aggressively at discount; a pullback with a strong sweep → expect a deeper pullback to premium.
- **Three liquidity entry models:** (1) limit at the refined POI after a sweep; (2) trail the candles in after the sweep; (3) wait for a CHoCH then enter the pullback (FVG / extreme zone / 0.618). More confirmation = lower risk but can miss the move — pick what fits your psychology and **test**.
- Sessions: Asian high/low often swept before the real move; trade **Frankfurt (7am UK) / London**; avoid high-impact news, the ~4pm GMT London close (counter-trend holds), and the 10pm GMT daily close (spread widening).

## 7. RISK MANAGEMENT

- **Risk 1% per trade, kept consistent** (compound up/down) — "impossible to blow an account." Warns hard against the beginner urge to risk 5%.
- **Drawdown math:** 5% risk + 6 losses = 26.5% DD; **50% DD needs +100% to recover;** 30%+ = danger.
- **Default 3:1 R:R**, set-and-forget, full size, no partials (though smaller targets like 2R can win more in live execution).
- **Expectancy = (avg win × win rate) − (avg loss × loss rate)** — win rate or R:R alone are meaningless. At **3R you only need 25% win rate** to break even.
- **Variance is baked in:** at 60% WR, ~98% chance of 7 losses in a row; at 40% WR, near-certain 10 in a row (per 1,000 trades). **Discount your backtested win rate by 5–10%** for live use.
- Free tools: an equity-curve Monte Carlo simulator and a prop-firm challenge optimizer.

### Prop-firm / funded angle (a major focus)
- Counter-intuitive: **don't be overly conservative.** Conservative (0.5%) ≈ 97–98% pass but 7–12 months/challenge; aggressive (3%) ≈ 50–67% pass but funded in **1–4 months**. The 3 hidden costs of going slow: opportunity cost, psychological pressure, more time = more chances to break a rule.
- **Only take challenges you can afford ≥3 attempts.** Never change risk mid-challenge.

## 8. ROADMAP TO PROFITABILITY

The development loop:
1. **Learn a mechanical strategy** (rules so tight emotion can't interfere).
2. **Build a mechanical trade plan** (what to do in every scenario, every timeframe, every level).
3. **Prove the edge with data** (backtest/forward-test → win rate, avg R, variance, max consecutive losses, max drawdown).
4. **Journal AND review** on a cadence: **day → week → month → quarter → year.** "It's acceptable to make mistakes; not to keep making the same ones."
5. **Close the performance discrepancy** (strategy's results vs your actual results; track trades taken *and missed*).
6. **Scale to funded capital**, keep it, compound.

The three "systems" that break the failure loop: **(1) rule-based trading, (2) data collection, (3) structured performance reviews** (pre-session / post-session / weekly-monthly-quarterly — the pitch for their "Photon OS" tool).

**Timeline reality:** people overestimate months, underestimate years. Progress is "three steps forward, two back" — judge by quarter and year, not week.

### The 9 daily habits
Religious morning routine ("half of trading is showing up") · pre-session analysis ≥30 min before open · session focus (no phone/social, medium TF only, drop to LTF last second) · post-session bar-replay + recorded recap · exercise (raise pain threshold) · disciplined diet (fasts through session) · ≥1 hr/day focused backtesting *with intention* · social interaction ≥30 min · consistent sleep (bed alarm). Don't install all at once — "master the art of showing up," then advance.

## 9. PSYCHOLOGY (their signature models)

- **Caveman-brain model:** a 200k-year-old brain built for an *immediate-return* world now in a *delayed-return* one. **Amygdala** (emotional, nanosecond pleasure/pain) vs **neocortex** (strategy). Under stress, info hits the amygdala first and **hijacks** the neocortex — and the brain **can't tell a real threat from the discomfort of open risk** ("fighting candlesticks instead of saber-tooth tigers"). Most classic mistakes (early break-even, FOMO, revenge size-ups, early profit-taking) are the subconscious **avoiding pain**.
- **Photon / polarity model:** losers swing between emotional poles (greed↔fear, elation↔depression) — Newton's 3rd law, chasing wins → harder crash on losses. The winning ~10–20% ("Masters") stay **calm, centered, neutral** — a **photon = a particle of light with no charge** (the brand metaphor). Drill: **show no reaction to wins or losses** — the only question is *did you follow your rules?*
- **Expectations vs reality:** the #1 blocker is **beliefs misaligned with reality** (fantasies about time-to-consistency and money). Don't put goals on a pedestal — **neutralize the fantasy by listing the goal's hidden negatives** ("wisdom is the instantaneous recognition of both sides"). Avoid the "10R/month" fantasy that makes you over-risk mid-month.
- **Discipline rules:** **max 3 losses/day then stop**; no celebration on wins, walk away 5 min on losses (don't tilt); feel the emotion, don't act; control *inputs* (analysis, showing up, taking every valid trade), not *outcomes*.
- **The 3-step problem-solving process:** (1) identify the *exact* problem (not "I'm not profitable" but "I take too many losses in NY session"); (2) ask "why" repeatedly to the root cause (FOMO → needing money → build other income, or scarcity → prove abundance with data); (3) implement a concrete rule (max-loss rule, ban counter-trend trades, trade London only).
