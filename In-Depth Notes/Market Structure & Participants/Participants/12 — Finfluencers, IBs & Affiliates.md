# 12 — Finfluencers, IBs & Affiliates

## What they are
This is the **customer-acquisition layer** that sits in front of brokers: **introducing brokers (IBs)**, **affiliates**, rebate sites, signal-sellers, paid "mentors", and social-media **finfluencers**. None of them clear or hold your money — their job is to *route people* to a broker in exchange for a payment.

## How they operate
Brokers pay these partners per referred trader through standard marketing structures: **affiliate / CPA (cost-per-acquisition)** — a flat bounty per funded account; **revenue-share** — an ongoing cut of the broker's revenue from your activity; and **rebate** — a kickback per lot traded. The partner's compensation is wired directly to onboarding more accounts and more trading, not to whether those accounts succeed.

## How they make money
Per-head and per-volume payments from the broker. A finfluencer or IB earns whether the referred trader wins or loses — and in some models earns *more* the more the trader loses.

## Why they matter / conflicts
The conflict is structural, not incidental. Much broker revenue scales with client **trading volume** and, in **B-book** models, directly with client **losses** (see [[08 — Brokers — the business model & conflicts]]). Because the acquisition layer is paid out of that revenue, its incentive is to **maximise sign-ups and activity**, not client outcomes. A "mentor" paid per funded account profits identically whether you blow up or compound — yet presents as an aligned guide.

**Honest caveat:** referral marketing is legal, disclosed, and ubiquitous across every industry. The red flag is the **undisclosed** conflict — the affiliate who never tells you a broker pays them, or the "educator" hiding a per-account bounty behind the language of mentorship.

Regulators have begun acting on exactly this. **SEBI's 2024 finfluencer regulations** restrict registered intermediaries (brokers, advisers) from associating with **unregistered** finfluencers — cutting the payment pipe between regulated firms and unlicensed promoters. **📄 sourced**.

## What it means for retail
Assume anyone steering you toward a specific broker, prop firm, or signal service is paid to do so until proven otherwise. Ask the single clarifying question — *"are you compensated if I sign up?"* — and treat hedged or evasive answers as the answer. Compensation per *account* or per *lot* is a direct conflict; compensation tied to *your* verified results is rare and worth noting.

## Limitations
Disclosure norms and legality vary by jurisdiction; the SEBI rules apply to India and to registered intermediaries, not to the global influencer market at large. Not every affiliate is predatory — the framework flags incentives, not individuals.
