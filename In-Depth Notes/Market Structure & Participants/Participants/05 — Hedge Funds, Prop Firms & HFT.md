# 05 — Hedge Funds, Prop Firms & HFT

## What they are
Three related but distinct fast-money participants:
- **Hedge funds** — pooled outside capital chasing absolute returns, typically charging "**2 and 20**" (≈2% of AUM plus 20% of profits), using leverage obtained through prime brokers (see [[09 — Leverage, Margin & Prime Brokers]]).
- **Prop (proprietary) firms** — trade the **firm's own capital**, not client money. No external investors to answer to.
- **HFT (high-frequency trading) firms** — a specialized prop subset whose entire edge is **speed and latency**: colocation next to exchange matching engines, microsecond reaction, massive order counts.

## How they trade / operate
Hedge funds run diverse strategies (long/short equity, macro, arbitrage, event-driven), often levered. Prop and HFT firms run automated, algorithmic strategies — market making, statistical arbitrage, latency arbitrage — and turn over positions in seconds. The common thread: they are sophisticated, well-capitalized, and technologically far ahead of any [[02 — Retail Traders|retail trader]].

## How they make money (their edge)
Speed, models, leverage, and information processing. The Indian data makes the edge undeniable. In **FY24, SEBI found** proprietary traders booked gross profits of **₹33,000 crore** and FPIs **₹28,000 crore** while individuals & others lost **over ₹61,000 crore** (before costs), and **96% of proprietary-trader profits and 97% of FPI profits came from algorithmic trading** (✅ SEBI Sep-2024, confirmed verbatim from the official annexure PDF: `sebi_data/attachdocs/sep-2024/1727086965496.pdf`). That is the speed/algo edge over retail, quantified — and it lines up with the verified ✅ SEBI loss-rate data ([[22 — How the Industry Profits from Retail Losses]]).

## Where they sit in the food chain
Top predators. They sit above retail, HNW, and even many slower institutions in *effective* edge — they are explicitly the counterparty extracting the gains that retail loses. See [[01 — The Market Food Chain]] and [[22 — How the Industry Profits from Retail Losses]].

## What it means for a retail trader
You are, statistically, on the losing side of these participants in derivatives. When you take a fast intraday options trade, the counterparty is frequently an algo that is faster, better-informed, and trading without emotion. The honest implication: avoid games (latency, micro-scalping) you cannot win, and compete only where your independence and patience matter.

## Limitations / honest caveats
"₹61,000 crore lost by individuals" is gross across the segment, not a per-trader figure, and prop/FPI profits include legitimate liquidity provision, not pure predation. The FY24 numbers are India-specific (F&O); other markets differ. Hedge funds also fail constantly — the survivors get headlines.
