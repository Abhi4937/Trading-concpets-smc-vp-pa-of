# 07 — Investment Banks (sell-side)

## What they are
Investment banks are the **"sell-side"** of the market — the firms that create, distribute, and trade securities and **sell** research and execution services to the buy-side 📄. They stand between the issuers of securities (companies, governments) and the [[04 — Institutions|institutions]] and [[05 — Hedge Funds, Prop Firms & HFT|hedge funds]] that buy them.

## How they trade / operate
A full-service bank runs several lines at once:
- **Underwriting** — bringing IPOs and bond issues to market.
- **M&A advisory** — advising on mergers, acquisitions, and restructurings.
- **Sales & trading** — facilitating client orders and trading the bank's book.
- **Research** — analysts publishing coverage distributed to clients.
- **Dealing / market making** — quoting and providing liquidity as principal (see [[06 — Market Makers & Dealers]]).

## How they make money (their edge)
Three sources: **fees** (underwriting and advisory mandates — the prestige, high-margin business), **spreads** (sales & trading, market making), and **information flow** — sitting at the center of capital markets, they see deal pipelines, client positioning, and order flow before almost anyone. Their edge is access, relationships, and being the indispensable intermediary for issuance.

## Where they sit in the food chain
They are the **sell-side counterpart to the buy-side**: the buy-side (institutions, hedge funds) *buys* research and execution; the sell-side (banks, brokers) *sells* it. They are upstream gatekeepers of new supply and central conduits of liquidity and information. See [[01 — The Market Food Chain]] and [[08 — Brokers — the business model & conflicts]].

## What it means for a retail trader
You almost never deal with an investment bank directly, but their footprints shape your world: their research moves prices when distributed to clients, their underwriting sets IPO supply, and their trading desks are part of the liquidity you trade into. Sell-side research is **marketing as much as analysis** — a "buy" rating can coexist with the bank earning fees from that same issuer.

## Limitations / honest caveats
The classic **research-vs-banking conflict** is real but heavily regulated post-2003 (Global Settlement) with mandated information barriers. Not all banks do everything — boutiques advise only on M&A; bulge-bracket firms do the full stack. "Sell-side" is a role label, not a judgment of quality; their research is often genuinely rigorous, just not disinterested.
