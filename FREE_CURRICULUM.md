# The Free, Legitimate Curriculum — Learn the Fractal Flow Pro Method Without the Paywall

*A self-study path that reaches the same destination as the FFP paid courses (FFS / MMS / NTS / PAT 1–3 + the four quant eBooks) using only free, lawful sources. Every FFP idea is a **relabel of an older, openly documented technique** — so the whole method is learnable from primary sources, your own repo, and the channels' free videos.*

> **Why this exists.** Cracked/leaked copies of paid courses are (a) copyright infringement and (b) the single most malware-laden download category on the web. The material itself is not proprietary knowledge — FFP's own free content admits most terms are "renamed century-old TA." This plan gets you the substance legally.
>
> **How to use it.** Your repo already contains the distilled method (`Fractal Flow Pro/CONCEPTS.md`) and the exact paid syllabus (`Fractal Flow Pro/Website Catalog/COURSES.md`). This file is the **bridge**: it sequences that knowledge into stages and points each FFP topic at a free resource that teaches the *original* technique. Read the repo synthesis first for the "what," then use the free sources here for the depth/"how."

## The core insight that makes this free

FFP's catalog is three layers wearing proprietary names:

| FFP product | What it actually is (open-source equivalent) |
|---|---|
| **FFS** (Fractal Flow Strategy) | Market-structure + price-action reversal trading (swing points, pullbacks, reversal candles, divergence) — classic **Larry Williams / Bulkowski / Wyckoff** material |
| **MMS** (Market Maker Strategy) | **ICT/Smart-Money-Concepts** liquidity & manipulation (sweeps, inducement, order blocks) — taught free in full by ICT himself |
| **NTS** (Newtonian Trading Strategy) | **Alan Andrews' Pitchfork / Median-Line + Action-Reaction** method — a 1960s course now in the public domain |
| **PAT 1–3** | The above three fused + geometry (vectors, Fib clusters) — **Andrews + Fibonacci + Gann-lite geometry** |
| **Volatility / Gamma / Vega eBooks** | Standard **option-Greeks & dealer-hedging** theory — free from Schwab, tastylive, Hull's textbook, SpotGamma |
| **Statistical Arbitrage by Cointegration** | **Engle–Granger cointegration pairs trading** — free QuantStart/academic tutorials + Python |

If you learn the right-hand column, you have learned the left-hand column.

## Stage 1 — Price Action & Market Structure (= the FFS core)

Maps to **FFS lectures 1–19** and **PAT 1 lectures 13–30** (extremes, midpoints, solid/fake structure, fractal/inside/outside/pressure bars).

### 1a. Candlesticks done *quantitatively* (not name-memorization)
FFP's whole candle stance — "read body vs tail, trade only statistically-validated patterns" — comes straight from Bulkowski.
- **[Bulkowski — Candlestick patterns index](https://thepatternsite.com/CandleEntry.html)** (free): identification + real performance stats per pattern.
- **[Bulkowski — Top 10 performing candles](https://www.thepatternsite.com/CandlePerformers.html)**: this is the source of FFP's "trade the hammer, not the inverted hammer" hierarchy (CONCEPTS §6).
- Repo: `In-Depth Notes/Fractal Flow Pro/06_FreeTradingCourses/...Candlestick Patterns/` already has worked notes.

### 1b. Swing structure & the inside-candle filter (FFP's "signature")
This is **Larry Williams**' material, openly taught.
- FFP's "fractal candle / one-bar reversal / hook / Oops / NR4-NR7" are all Larry Williams + Toby Crabel terms. Search free write-ups of: *"Larry Williams Oops signal," "inside bar / hikkake pattern," "NR7."*
- Repo anchor: `CONCEPTS.md` §2 (strong vs weak structure, BOS/MSS/CHoCH, the 4 reversal types).

### 1c. Divergence (FFS lectures 11–14: simple / reversal / hybrid)
- Standard RSI divergence (regular + hidden). Any free RSI-divergence tutorial covers FFP's "fractal reversal/hybrid divergence" — it's divergence read at a swing point.
- FFP tip worth keeping: smooth RSI with a short Hull MA; draw trendlines on the RSI line itself.

**Exit test:** on replay, identify a pullback to structure + a Bulkowski-validated reversal candle + a divergence, and write the trade.

Maps to **MMS lectures 1–10** (liquidity pools, wash lines, press zones, frequency lines, manipulation process). FFP openly says this is renamed ICT/SMC — and **ICT teaches the whole thing free**.

- **ICT 2022 Mentorship** (free, on YouTube — Michael Huddleston). The canonical free SMC course. Covers liquidity sweeps, order blocks, FVG, MSS, displacement, premium/discount.
  - Overview/guide: [ICT Mentorship 2022 explainer](https://crypoptionhub.com/ict-mentorship-2022/)
  - Condensed: ["Every ICT concept in 17 minutes"](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QQijkhheJ9g) · ["Liquidity, FVG & Order Blocks"](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QrYW_qzWmrg)
  - Free aggregators: [ICT Flow](https://ictflow.com/)
- **Translate as you go** using `CONCEPTS.md` §3 (FFP's contrarian take): FFP keeps the ICT vocabulary but reframes FVG = "hidden Low Volume Node," sweep vs grab vs run vs inducement, OTE = Fib 0.62–0.79, order block = open of the sweep candle. The key FFP *disagreement* to internalize: **"price seeks value, liquidity is only fuel"** — don't treat liquidity as a magnet.
- Repo: `Photon Trading/CONCEPTS.md` is a second, more mechanical SMC treatment of the same ideas — use it as a cross-check.

**Exit test:** annotate a chart with a liquidity sweep → MSS → return to order block/FVG → entry, and explain why the sweep induced the move.

## Stage 3 — Andrews Pitchfork & Geometry (= the NTS course, FFP's flagship tool)

Maps to **NTS lectures 1–15** and **PAT 1 lectures 31–47** (line theory, anchoring, extension, vector/action-space extrapolation). This is the **one genuinely distinctive FFP tool**, and it's a **public-domain 1960s method**.

- **[Andrews-Lines.com](https://www.andrews-lines.com/)** — dedicated free site teaching Alan Andrews' pitchfork and median-line method.
- **[Andrews "Action-Reaction" / Median Line course (PDF)](https://dl.abcbourse.ir/dl/Library/book/using%20median%20lines%20an%20emipirical%20study.pdf)** — "Using Median Lines As A Trading Tool: An Empirical Study." This is the empirical backbone behind FFP's "~80% reach the median line" and Hagopian's Rule.
- [Andrews' Pitchfork primer PDF (MQL5)](https://c.mql5.com/3/132/Andrews__Pitchfork.pdf) · [AFTA pitchfork deck](http://www.afta-dfw.com/docs/Andrews%20PITCHFORKS-AFTAFinal.pdf) · [Morge — 5 median-line principles (IBKR webinar PDF)](https://www.interactivebrokers.com/webinars/WB_1466_CME_Morge_Five_Median_lines.pdf)
- [STOCATA free video lesson on the pitchfork](http://stocata.org/youtube/video_026.html) · [MQL5 article on Andrews' methods](https://www.mql5.com/en/articles/12140)
- Repo anchor: `CONCEPTS.md` §5 (construction P0/P1/P2, Schiff/modified/pendulum variants, Hagopian's Rule, drawing the fork on the RSI line) and §8 (NTS = Newton's 1st/3rd law → action/reaction symmetry targeting).
- **NTS = Andrews + Newton's framing.** "Action-Reaction symmetry" is literally Andrews' action/reaction lines; "Newton's 1st law = trend inertia" is narrative. Learn Andrews properly and NTS is yours.

**Exit test:** draw a correct standard fork on 3 charts, convert one to a Schiff when the slope is too steep, and place a trade off a median-line + frequency-line + fractal-candle confluence.

## Stage 6 — The "Real Science" Edge: Cointegration Stat-Arb (= the 161-page eBook)

Maps to *Statistical Arbitrage by Cointegration* — FFP's one endorsed *scientific* strategy (CONCEPTS §11). Pure stats, fully documented in the open literature.

- **Method:** Engle–Granger — regress two non-stationary series, ADF-test the residual; if stationary, the pair is cointegrated → mean-revert the spread (enter at |z| > 2).
- Free resources:
  - [MQL5 — Engle-Granger & Johansen cointegration tests (with code)](https://www.mql5.com/en/articles/18702)
  - [Portfolio Optimization Book — Statistical Arbitrage chapter (free online)](https://portfoliooptimizationbook.com/book/15.7-statarb.html) · [pairs-trading slides PDF](https://portfoliooptimizationbook.com/slides/slides-pairs-trading.pdf)
  - QuantStart's cointegration/pairs-trading tutorials (free articles on quantstart.com) + `statsmodels` `coint()` in Python.
  - Background: Engle & Granger won the 2003 Nobel — the primary papers are free.

**Exit test:** in Python, test two related tickers for cointegration and backtest a z-score mean-reversion entry/exit.

## Stage 7 — Risk, Math & Position Sizing (FFS lectures 20–25 — non-negotiable)

FFP is rigorous here and it's all standard, free math. Repo: `CONCEPTS.md` §9 is a complete summary.

- Break-even win rate = **1 / (1 + R:R)** (1:3 → 25%); expectancy = Win%·AvgWin − Loss%·AvgLoss; drawdown asymmetry (−50% needs +100%).
- Sizing: fixed-fractional (preferred), ATR/volatility, fractional/half **Kelly**, Optimal-F. Martingale = warning only.
- Rules: risk **0.5–1%**, always stop *and* target, never widen a stop, never move a target.
- Free depth: BabyPips risk-management grade; Van Tharp's "expectancy/R-multiple" articles; any free Kelly-criterion explainer.

**Exit test:** compute expectancy and worst-case drawdown for your own win-rate/R:R before risking real money.

## Stage 8 — Psychology & the 38 Biases (FFS lectures 26–64)

FFP's "38 psychological traps" are just **named cognitive biases** — every one is free on Wikipedia/standard psych sources. The list is in `COURSES.md` (FFS lecture 27–64).

- Read each bias (Prospect Theory, Hyperbolic Discounting, Disposition Effect, Overconfidence, Gambler's/Hot-Hand Fallacy, Dunning-Kruger, etc.) and write one line on how it shows up in *your* trading.
- FFP's flagship framings to keep (CONCEPTS §10): the **Spectral Point of No Return** (overconfidence after a winning streak is more dangerous than losses), **hyperbolic discounting kills R:R** (don't close winners early), "you don't need Spartan discipline, just enough to follow a written plan."
- Free books worth it: *Trading in the Zone* (Mark Douglas) summaries; Kahneman *Thinking, Fast and Slow* (the source for half the bias list).

## Putting it together — the integrated method (PAT 1–3)

PAT is FFS + MMS + NTS fused with "trade at the intersection." Once Stages 1–4 + 7–8 are solid, the integration *is* the practice:

1. Map structure (Stage 1) → 2. Locate where liquidity/value sits (Stages 2 & 4) → 3. Find geometric confluence (Stage 3: fork line + frequency line + Fib cluster) → 4. **Demand a reaction** (fractal candle) at the intersection → 5. Enter next-candle open, stop beyond solid structure, target ≥1:3 → 6. Size by Stage 7, behave by Stage 8.

> FFP's own admission (CONCEPTS §11): the free content is strong on *concepts and critique* but withholds some mechanical rules behind the paywall — and the proprietary terms are coinages for older ideas. **The free sources above teach those older ideas directly**, so you lose the branding, not the substance.
