## 0. The Big Picture — Their Philosophy

Fractal Flow Pro is an **intellectual / "first-principles" price-action school**. They reject mechanical systems and indicator-worship, and instead build a discretionary method on physics, statistics, market microstructure, and behavioral finance. Two ideas run through everything:

1. **Integration / "trading at the intersection" (their #1 principle).** No single technique is tradeable alone. You stack 2–4 **lag-free** techniques that converge at the *same price and time* — structure + a price-action reaction + divergence + a geometric tool (pitchfork/VWAP/Fib cluster). More convergence = higher probability. *"The holy grail does not exist."*
2. **Price is fractal ("fractal flow").** The same highs-within-highs behavior repeats on every timeframe (so "the timeframe doesn't matter"). Big, reliable reversals occur when the **major flow and minor flow align**.

Their worldview (the "REAL" framing that makes them contrarian):
- **Price seeks VALUE, not liquidity.** Liquidity is fuel/mechanism, not a magnet or destination. *"A swimmer doesn't swim toward water, he swims through it."*
- **Price is "the tip of the iceberg"** — a lossy shadow of the real market (Plato's cave). The market is a multi-layer arena of speculators, arbitrageurs, hedgers, market makers, HFTs — not a binary smart-money-vs-retail fight. Setups fail because of off-chart participants (OTC, dark pools, hedging), **not because you're on the wrong timeframe.**
- **"Smart money" = the CFTC COT commercials (hedgers),** not chart-pattern gurus. Manipulation is **emergent** behavior (bird-flocking), not one master algorithm.
- **Technical analysis with lagging indicators is a "pseudoscience"** ("TA is to econometrics what astrology is to astronomy"). Price is **non-stationary, stochastic, heteroskedastic** — the hardest time series — so fixed-period indicators on non-linear price are *"a screwdriver to paint a wall."* The meaningful information lives in the noise that indicators smooth away.

## 1. THE UNIVERSAL TRADE TEMPLATE (identical across every course)

1. **Map structure** — count highs/lows first. Uptrend = HH+HL, downtrend = LH+LL, range = flat.
2. **Be a PULLBACK trader, never a breakout trader.** Breakout traders are the prey of traps; pullbacks give smaller stops, precise entries, bigger targets.
3. **Wait for a pullback to a logical level** (S/R, trendline, supply/demand zone, Fib cluster, pitchfork line, pattern completion).
4. **Require confluence (≥2, ideally 3–4 lag-free confirmations).**
5. **Demand a REACTION at the level** — *"the reaction to the level is more important than the level itself."* The reaction shows as a **fractal candle** (their term: high-volatility reversal bar with a long opposite tail, often an outside bar — overlaps pin bar / hammer), an inside candle, or a volatility burst.
6. **Entry:** at the **open of the candle after** the reaction/fractal candle; OR a stop order **one tick beyond an inside candle** (the "hook").
7. **Stop-loss:** just beyond the logical extreme (the solid structure / reaction-candle tail). **Never widen a stop.**
8. **Target:** standard **1:3** (up to 1:4–1:6 on high-conviction multi-layer setups). Or trail via a trendline/structure for runners.
9. **Risk 0.5–1% per trade.** Let price hit the stop or the target — no early exits.

## 2. MARKET STRUCTURE (the foundation)

- **Swing high** = candle with a lower high on both sides; **swing low** = higher low on both sides. "All significant reversals are swings, but not all swings are significant" → many false signals.
- **Larry Williams inside-candle filter (their signature):** a swing point formed by an *inside candle* to the right is unreliable; confirm a swing high only when price breaks the **lower** boundary of the swing candle's range (wick break = aggressive, close = conservative). Break of the upper boundary instead = false swing.
- **Strong vs weak structure (core distinction):** a Break of Structure (BOS) makes the **origin of the breaking move STRONG** and the **broken extreme WEAK**. In an uptrend, highs are *weak* structure (breaking them isn't necessarily significant); **lows are strong structure** — breaking a strong low is the meaningful event. Put stops behind *strong* extremes.
- **CHoCH (change of character)** = the market changing state (trend↔range). **MSS (market structure shift)** = violating an extreme that "shouldn't" break. Warning: **consecutive MSS are common and will trap you** — it's not a holy grail.
- **True vs false breakout:** a HH that *closes* above the prior high = solid structure; a HH that fails to close above = "fake structure" = a bull/bear trap.
- **Fractals & sub-structure:** major (swing) structure sets trend + stops; minor (sub) structure inside the pullback reveals **liquidity inducement** (the renamed "trap"). Markets are **multifractal** (roughness changes over time — Mandelbrot), which is *why* rigid rules fail.
- **4 reversal types:** failure swing/pullback, non-failure swing/expanding pivot (trap), double top/bottom, climax/V (the one reversal with no MSS warning — drop a timescale to catch it).

## 3. "REAL" SMART MONEY CONCEPTS & LIQUIDITY

They teach the ICT vocabulary but reframe it as renamed century-old TA, and insist it's **distorted/incomplete**, not mechanical:
- **Order taxonomy:** Market orders (aggressive, consume liquidity, initiate moves, invisible till executed), Limit (passive, provide liquidity, absorb, deceptive — can be cancelled), Stop (invisible until triggered, then become market orders → the source of "latent liquidity").
- **Latent liquidity** = clusters of stop orders (buy-stops above highs, sell-stops below lows) — it emerges from **predictable retail behavior** at any popular technique (trendlines, obvious Fibs, round numbers, pattern breakouts). Equal highs/lows = deeper pools.
- **Sweep** (deep fake breakout that fails) vs **grab** (fast probe + rejection) vs **run** (takes liquidity and continues = true breakout) vs **inducement** (deliberate counter-move to fuel continuation).
- **Liquidity voids:** explicit = a real gap; **implicit = Fair Value Gap** (gap between candle-1 and candle-3) — "a misleading name"; it works because it hides a **Low Volume Node**; price returning to it is *one possibility, not a rule*.
- **FVG / OTE / Order Block (when they do use them):** longs in **discount**, shorts in **premium**; **OTE** = Fib 0.62–0.79 (midpoint ~0.705); **order block** = open of the large candle that sweeps liquidity then breaks structure, mean-threshold = its 50%.
- **Heatmap / order-flow tools:** liquidity walls, stacked walls, withdrawals, flips; read **absorption vs aggression** (price stalls at a wall = absorption > aggression; price zooms through = continuation). Prefer **futures** (centralized) for clean order-flow data; live DOM is largely "a vain exercise" (spoofing, icebergs, hidden OTC volume).

## 4. SUPPLY & DEMAND (institutional)

- **Why zones exist:** institutions can't fill huge orders at once (slippage, front-running, liquidity shock) → they **split orders** over time, leaving accumulation/distribution footprints = S/D zones. Treat all institutional activity as one **composite operator** (Wyckoff), justified as emergent, not centralized.
- **Zone types:** reversal = Drop-Base-Rally (demand) / Rally-Base-Drop (supply); continuation = Rally-Base-Rally / Drop-Base-Drop.
- **Identify (2 steps):** (1) find a significant move (high body-percentage candles = certainty); (2) find the **base** it came from (low body-percentage).
- **Drawing (risk variants):** whole swing-candle range (high-risk) → ignore the shadow (medium) → only the shadow (low-risk, tighter stop but may not fill). Order-flow version: footprint value area / stacked imbalance / 40% volume-profile value area / **anchored VWAP** on the swing (institutions benchmark to VWAP; pullbacks end at the VWAP line or its 2σ band).
- **Qualifiers (guidelines, not rules):** strong departure, short/narrow base, confluence, **freshness** (untested first-touch best), originality, nearby inducement, VWAP benchmarking, nesting. Broken demand → reliable supply (switching quality).
- **R:R is non-linear:** halving a 1:3 stop → +133% R/R; at 1:7 you can lose 7 straight and break even.

## 5. ANDREWS PITCHFORK (their single most-promoted tool — "zero lag, rooted in physics")

- **Construction:** 3 alternating, *most-recent-and-significant* extremes (P0/P1/P2) → a **median/center line** + equidistant upper/lower parallels; price is "trapped" between the parallels.
- **Variants:** Standard; **Schiff / Modified Schiff** (move the A-axis to the midpoint of A–B to flatten a too-steep fork — convert when the angle is >45°); frequency-shifted (move position, not slope, when frequency changes); pendulum (rapid low-high-low for minor reversals); negative-extrapolation forks.
- **Median-line theory:** in the *Pitchfork* playlist they deliberately avoid the classic "~80% reach the median" claim and describe it qualitatively (price exhausts at the center line and reverses). *However, in the Trading-Strategies playlist they do state ~**80% probability** price reaches the pitchfork center line once the minor flow starts.* **Hagopian's Rule:** failure to reach a fork line predicts a stronger move the other way (use only with confluence).
- **Setup:** confluence of fork line + horizontal frequency line + a price-action bar (hybrid/fractal/inside) → enter at the next candle's open (aggressive: limit at the intersection); **stop at a solid extreme** (not jammed tight — noise will take it); **target ≥1:3.**
- **Edge claim (Better Side of TA):** the pitchfork caught a reversal **14 days / 350 pips earlier** than a 10/20 MA crossover, and nailed a level a MACD crossover missed by 45 pips. Can also be drawn **on the RSI line**.

## 6. CANDLESTICKS & "FAST" PRICE-ACTION PATTERNS

- **Don't memorize Japanese names — read body vs tail.** Universal rule: enter next-candle open, stop beyond the pattern extreme, target 3× stop.
- **Tradeable (per Bulkowski stats):** hammer, engulfing, belt-hold, morning/evening star, abandoned baby, piercing/dark cloud, three soldiers, rising/falling three methods. **Weak / don't trade alone:** inverted hammer, hanging man, harami (~50/50), doji family. (They explicitly say *"use the hammer instead of the inverted hammer."*)
- **Candle anatomy tools:** **candle stochastic** (where the close sits in the range = strength/weakness) and **body-to-range ratio** (certainty vs indecision).
- **Western/fast patterns:** gaps (breakaway = best, runaway = measuring, exhaustion = fades), spike/wide-range bars (open = future S/R), one-bar reversal (= their fractal bar), pipe, horn, inside bar, hook, hikkake (trade the *failure* of an inside-bar break), Larry Williams "Oops", NR4/NR7, opening-range breakout.
- **Contrarian ICT take:** spikes / FVGs / order blocks are *"just shifts in volatility and ordinary S/R"*; the **open of a high-volatility candle** (their "volatility shift" / "point of ignition") is the level that matters.

## 7. THE OTHER TOOLS (with their "secrets")

- **Support/Resistance** (their "most reliable" technique): active & lag-free; switching quality (S↔R flip); more tests = stronger; can be horizontal, sloped, or curved (1.618 Fib arc); also drawn on the RSI line ("momentum S/R").
- **Fibonacci:** use only the simple, most-watched ratios (0.382, 0.618, 1.618, 2.618); works via **self-fulfilling prophecy** (no scientific basis). Their preferred method = **Fib clusters** (overlap of multiple Fib tools, e.g. 50% retr ≈ 161.8% proj) + a reaction.
- **Moving averages:** all lag; **Hull MA is "least-bad"**; the lag-reduction trick is to watch the **MA's slope (rate of change)** which turns before the MA; best use is **dynamic S/R** and the **Fractal Crossover** (10/20 HMA, higher-TF pre-signal then lower-TF signal). Optimal crossover periods are *unknowable* (regime-dependent) — the killer flaw.
- **Bollinger Bands:** a volatility model; use the **empirical rule** (2σ≈95%, 3σ≈99.7%) and wait for a **close beyond 3σ** for higher-probability mean-reversion — but mean-reversion **fails in trends** ("moving goalposts").
- **Volume / VSA (their respected volume method):** *volume confirms price; the edge is the violations.* VSA 2×2: wide+high = continuation, wide+low = reversal at a barrier, narrow+low = rest, **narrow+high = absorption/reversal**. Tools: histogram, VPOC, anchored VWAP (their favorite — "volume-adjusted non-linear Bollinger Bands" without the rolling-window flaw), CVD (exhaustion/absorption divergence), footprint stacked imbalances (≥300% diagonal).
- **RSI:** use **divergence** (reversal & continuation/hidden) — never alone, timing is unpredictable; smooth the RSI with a 20-HMA source; draw trendlines/channels/pitchforks on the RSI itself.
- **Market/Volume Profile & Auction Market Theory:** balance = ranging = acceptance = high volume (attracts); imbalance = trending = rejection = low volume (repels). POC, value area (70%), naked POC, **80% rule**, profile shapes P/b/D. Market Profile (TPO) adds Initial Balance, single prints, day types.
- **GEX (gamma exposure):** dealers hedge to stay delta-neutral → **positive gamma suppresses volatility (mean-reverting); negative gamma amplifies it (trending)**. **Call Resistance** (price decelerates/rejects), **Put Support** (price accelerates/bounces), **gamma flip** = breakout. *"Gamma determines HOW price approaches a level; option flow determines WHETHER it holds."*

## 8. THE NAMED STRATEGIES (Trading Strategies playlist)

One discretionary price-action system sold as stackable sub-strategies:
- **FFS — Fractal Flow Strategy (core):** fractal candle at solid structure / S-D zone with ≥2 confirmations → enter next open; stop beyond nearest solid structure; target ≥1:3.
- **MMS — Market Maker Strategy:** spot MM "signatures" (organized mechanical candles, **poking** a level without closing beyond, expanding pivots, von-Restorff visual anomaly), trade *against the obvious* after the poke triggers retail stops and reverses.
- **NTS — Newtonian Trading Strategy (most advanced):** Newton's 1st law (trend inertia) + 3rd law (action/reaction symmetry). Enter on pitchfork/frequency-line touches; **target via Action/Reaction symmetry** (mirror the action range to the opposite side) or the pitchfork center line (~80% hit).
- **Flow Paradox:** minor flow briefly trumps the major trend (press line → ignition point) → early counter-trend reversal, targets up to ~1:6.
- **Multilayer (the flagship):** triple convergence FFS + MMS + NTS + divergence. They even teach **two-sided trades** (simultaneous long+short when ambiguous) since one 1:3 winner covers the stopped side.
- *No win-rate/backtest numbers are ever given* — they emphasize consistency through R:R and precise reading, using only ~last 200 candles and a single timeframe.

## 9. RISK MANAGEMENT (rigorous and math-heavy)

- **The 6 parameters:** Capital → %Risk ("f") → Dollar Risk → Stop Size → Trade Size → R:R. *You can't size a trade without a stop → you can't trade without a stop.*
- **Formulas they teach:** break-even win rate = **1/(1+R:R)** (1:3 → 25%); expectancy = WinRate×AvgWin − LossRate×AvgLoss; P(n losses) = lossrate^n; drawdown asymmetry (−50% needs +100%). Worked proof: 200 trades, 30% win, 1:3 → +$4,000 despite being wrong 70%.
- **Hard rules:** risk **0.5–1%** (never >1%); always stop AND target; **never widen a stop, never move a target;** let the market hit one.
- **Sizing models:** fixed-fractional (preferred), volatility/ATR, fixed-ratio, **fractional/half Kelly**, Optimal-F. **Martingale is taught only as a warning** (gambler's fallacy; needs infinite capital; "never use it").
- **Risk is conserved, only transformed** (capital ↔ positional ↔ target risk) — techniques: roll-to-break-even, scale-out = S/(S+P)×size, "risk collapse" (contract/expand risk so different stop sizes mean the same mathematically).

## 10. PSYCHOLOGY & MARKET WISDOM

- **The tripod:** technical analysis + risk management + psychology must work together; most traders fail by obsessing over technicals alone.
- **The brain takes the *quickest* decision, not the best;** improvising hands control to the intuitive mode "which is ignorant about trading." Cognitive biases are a ~200k-year inheritance you can only *manage*, not remove.
- **Spectral Point of No Return (flagship concept):** emotions sit on a spectrum with a point of no return on **both** ends. Overconfidence after a winning streak is *more* dangerous than handling losses — it "devastates more than 90% of traders."
- **You don't need Spartan discipline** — just enough to follow a pre-written plan.
- **Hyperbolic discounting** (closing winners early) destroys the R:R math — resist it.
- **Markets are second-order chaotic** (predictions change the system). A ~$100M tilt on EUR/USD (depth ~$100–200M/pip) can spark a $20–40B, 200–300 pip move — initial conditions set by market makers cascade nonlinearly (Lorenz attractor).
- **EMH is practically wrong:** price has an **objective** dimension (nominal price, where EMH holds) and a **subjective** dimension (each trader's value interpretation); the **discrepancy between them drives price**, which is why purely mechanical setups can't work.
- **Establish trend with multi-fractal analysis:** wait for **≥2 fractals** to set bias; when major + minor flows agree → strong move; when they conflict → don't fight the major flow. Trade major reversals or minor continuations.
- **Forex robots:** ~99.9% are static rule-based systems doomed in a dynamic market (Bonini's paradox); no general AI exists; "the only neural net up to the job is in your head."
